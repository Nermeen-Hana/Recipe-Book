## Recipe 1 — Savory Cottage Cheese & Cheddar Scrambled Eggs 🍳

**1 Day · Rest Day Breakfast · High Protein · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · Savory · Stovetop**

---

### Ingredients

🥚 Great Value Large White Eggs — 100g (2 large eggs)

🧀 Great Value 4% Cottage Cheese ⚠️ SAVORY ONLY — 113g (½ cup)

🧀 Great Value Fat-Free Shredded Cheddar — 14g

🍅 Roma Tomato — 80g, diced

🫑 Green Bell Pepper — 60g, finely diced

🧂 Great Value Iodized Salt — 1 pinch

🫙 Badia Ground Black Pepper — to taste

🌿 Dried Thyme — ¼ tsp

🥄 Great Value Butter Cooking Spray — 1–2 sprays

---

### Directions

**Prep**

🔪 Crack eggs into a bowl. Add cottage cheese and whisk together until combined but slightly textured — do not over-blend.

🔪 Dice the Roma tomato and green bell pepper. Pat dry with a paper towel to prevent watery eggs.

**Cook**

🔥 Heat the 4-cup egg pan over medium-low heat. Coat with 1–2 sprays of butter cooking spray.

🔥 Pour in the egg and cottage cheese mixture. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme. Let sit undisturbed for 30 seconds until the edges just begin to set.

🔥 Using a silicone spatula, gently fold the eggs from the outside in — slow, wide folds. Cook for 2–3 minutes total until just barely set and still glossy. Do not overcook.

🔥 In the final 30 seconds, scatter the fat-free shredded cheddar over the top and fold once more to melt.

**Finish**

✅ Slide onto a plate. Top with diced Roma tomato and green bell pepper — serve raw for crunch and acidity contrast.

✅ Season with a final crack of black pepper and serve immediately.

---

### Storage

❄️ Best eaten fresh. Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 1 day.

♨️ Reheat gently in the egg pan over low heat with a spray of cooking spray. Do not microwave — eggs turn rubbery.

---

### Nutrition ✅ Confirmed

🔥 Calories — 279 kcal

🥩 Protein — 31g

🍞 Carbs — 11g

🥑 Fat — 12.5g

🥦 Fiber — 2.3g

🧂 Sodium — 606mg ✅

---

### Nutrition Notes

🧂 Sodium comes primarily from cottage cheese (380mg) and cheddar — well above the 500mg meal floor.

🥑 Fat is mostly from whole eggs — good quality source of choline and B vitamins.

🩸 Eggs provide iron support — a positive contribution given low-normal ferritin status.

💊 Choline from 2 whole eggs (~250mg) supports neurological function and BRCA1 cellular repair pathways.

## Recipe 5 — Savory Egg, Mozzarella & Veggie Omelet 🍳

**1 Day · Rest Day Breakfast · High Protein · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · Savory · 4 Cup Egg Pan**

---

### Ingredients

🥚 Great Value Large White Eggs — 150g (3 large eggs)

🧀 Great Value Shredded Mozzarella — 14g

🧀 Great Value Fat-Free Shredded Cheddar — 14g

🫑 Green Bell Pepper — 80g, finely diced

🍅 Roma Tomato — 62g, diced and patted dry

🧂 Great Value Iodized Salt — 1 pinch

🫙 Badia Ground Black Pepper — to taste

🫙 Smoked Paprika — ¼ tsp

🥄 Great Value Butter Cooking Spray — 1–2 sprays

---

### Directions

**Prep**

🔪 Crack all 3 eggs into a bowl. Season with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika. Beat with a fork until fully combined and slightly frothy — about 30 seconds.

🔪 Dice bell pepper and tomato. Pat tomato dry with a paper towel to prevent a watery omelet.

🔪 Mix mozzarella and fat-free cheddar together in a small bowl. Set aside.

**Cook**

🔥 Heat the 4-cup egg pan over medium-low heat. Coat with 1–2 sprays of butter cooking spray.

🔥 Add diced green bell pepper. Sauté for 2 minutes until slightly softened. Push to the side.

🔥 Pour the beaten eggs into the pan over the peppers. Let sit undisturbed for 60 seconds until the edges are set but the center is still glossy.

🔥 Scatter the cheese blend across one half of the omelet. Spoon diced tomato over the cheese.

🔥 Using a spatula, fold the bare half of the omelet over the filled half. Cook 30–45 seconds more until the cheese is melted and the egg is just set — not browned on the outside.

**Finish**

✅ Slide onto a plate. Let rest 30 seconds before cutting — this allows the cheese to settle.

✅ Season with a final crack of black pepper and serve immediately.

---

### Storage

❄️ Best eaten fresh. Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 1 day.

♨️ Reheat in the egg pan over low heat with a spray of cooking spray for 1–2 minutes. Do not microwave.

---

### Nutrition ✅ Confirmed

🥩 Protein — 27g

🍞 Carbs — 7g

🥑 Fat — 18g ⚠️ Slightly above rest day breakfast fat target of 10–14g

🥦 Fiber — 2.3g

🧂 Sodium — 391mg ⚠️ Below 500mg — add an extra pinch of salt or pair with a sodium-containing beverage

---

### Nutrition Notes

⚠️ Fat at 18g exceeds the 10–14g rest day breakfast fat guideline. This comes entirely from whole eggs — a natural, high-quality fat source. To reduce: replace 1 whole egg with 2 egg whites. This flag is informational — no action required unless daily fat targets are being exceeded.

🧂 Sodium at 391mg is below the 500mg meal floor. Add a pinch of salt and pair with a NIDO coffee to bring total morning sodium toward target.

🩸 Whole eggs are a significant source of choline and non-heme iron — a positive contribution for low-normal ferritin.

🫀 BRCA1 note: saturated fat in this meal is low — egg yolks contribute mostly unsaturated fats. No concern at this portion.

🛋️ Rest Day Breakfast confirmed — 300 kcal fits the 280–320 kcal target. Protein at 27g is within the 25–30g range ✅.

## Recipe 12 — Cheesy Tuna & Egg Scramble 🍳

**1 Day · Gym Day Breakfast · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · 4 Cup Egg Pan**

---

### Ingredients

🥚 Great Value Large White Eggs — 150g (3 large)

🐟 Great Value Chunk Light Tuna in Water — 112g drained (2 cans)

🧀 Great Value Fat-Free Shredded Cheddar — 28g

🍋 Lemon juice — 5ml (1 tsp) ⚠️ Estimated

🧂 Great Value Iodized Salt — ¼ tsp

🫙 Badia Ground Black Pepper — ¼ tsp

🫙 Badia Ground Cumin — ¼ tsp

🧅 Red Onions — 30g, finely diced

🍅 Roma Tomato — 62g, diced

🥬 Romaine Lettuce — 85g (serve alongside)

🫒 Black olives — 20g, sliced ⚠️ Estimated

🥄 Great Value Butter Cooking Spray — 2 seconds spray

---

### Directions

**Prep (3 min)**

🐟 Open and drain tuna cans thoroughly — press with a fork to remove all water, then flake loosely.

🥚 Crack eggs into a bowl — add salt, pepper, cumin, and lemon juice — whisk vigorously for 30 seconds until fully combined and slightly frothy.

🧅 Dice red onion finely — set aside.

**Cook (5–6 min)**

🔥 Heat 4-cup egg pan over medium-low heat — spray with Great Value Butter Cooking Spray.

🧅 Add red onion — cook 2 minutes stirring until softened and translucent.

🥚 Pour egg mixture into the pan — let sit undisturbed for 20 seconds until edges just begin to set.

🥄 Using a spatula, gently push cooked egg from the edges toward the center — tilt pan to let uncooked egg flow into the empty spaces.

🐟 When eggs are about 70% set and still glossy — gently fold in drained tuna and shredded cheddar — fold 2–3 times to combine.

🔥 Remove pan from heat immediately — residual heat will finish cooking the eggs.

**Finish**

✅ Transfer to a plate — arrange romaine lettuce and sliced olives alongside.

🍅 Scatter diced Roma tomato over the scramble or alongside lettuce.

✅ Serve immediately.

---

### Storage

❄️ Not recommended for make-ahead — eggs become rubbery when refrigerated and reheated.

♨️ If unavoidable: store up to 1 day refrigerated in airtight container, reheat gently in microwave 30–40 seconds on 60% power with a splash of water.

### Nutrition Notes

🐟 Tuna (112g drained) provides ~22g protein at ~100 kcal — the most calorie-efficient protein source in the pantry.

🥚 3 eggs add 18g protein plus choline, B12, and vitamin D — critical for muscle repair on gym days.

## Recipe 44 — Crispy Cheese & Veggie Air Fryer Rice Paper Rolls 🌯

**🛋️ Rest Day · Breakfast · High Protein · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · Air Fryer**

> ⚠️ **Key technique:** Always use 2 sheets of rice paper per roll (double-wrap). A single sheet is too fragile and will tear or burst in the air fryer.

### Ingredients

**Filling:**
- 🥚 Great Value Large White Eggs — 100g (2 large, scrambled)
- 🧀 Great Value Shredded Mozzarella — 42g
- 🧀 Great Value Fat-Free Shredded Cheddar — 28g
- 🫒 Great Value Stuffed Manzanilla Olives — 20g, sliced into rounds
- 🟢 Green Bell Pepper — 60g, finely diced
- 🍅 Roma Tomato — 62g, finely diced and patted very dry
- 🧂 Great Value Iodized Salt — ¼ tsp
- 🫙 Badia Ground Black Pepper — ¼ tsp

**Wrapping:**
- 🌯 Star Anise Foods Vietnamese White Rice Spring Roll Wrapper — 12 wrappers (6 rolls × 2 sheets each)
- 🥄 Great Value Butter Cooking Spray — 3–4 sprays

### Directions

**Prep — filling first**

1. Finely dice green bell pepper and Roma tomato. Pat tomato pieces thoroughly dry with paper towels — excess moisture is the enemy of crispy rolls. Set aside.
2. Slice olives into thin rounds. Set aside.
3. Beat both eggs with salt and pepper. Scramble in a non-stick pan over medium-low heat using Great Value Butter Cooking Spray — cook until just set and still slightly glossy. Remove from heat immediately.
4. While eggs are still warm, stir in mozzarella and cheddar directly in the pan. Fold in diced bell pepper, tomato, and olive rounds. Stir gently to combine.
5. ⏳ Spread filling onto a plate and let it cool for 10 minutes before wrapping. Warm filling makes the rice paper tear.

**Wrap — double sheet method**

6. Fill a wide shallow bowl with cold water. Dip one rice paper sheet in the water and rotate slowly for 8–10 seconds until just pliable.
7. Lay the first sheet flat on a lightly damp cutting board. Immediately dip a second sheet for 8–10 seconds and lay it directly on top of the first.
8. Place approximately 2–3 tablespoons of cooled filling onto the lower third of the double sheet.
9. Fold the bottom edge up over the filling. Fold both sides inward. Roll tightly upward to seal. Press the seam gently to stick.
10. Repeat to make 6 rolls total. Keep them spaced apart on a lightly greased plate.

**Air fry**

11. Preheat air fryer to 200°C / 390°F for 3 minutes.
12. Spray the air fryer basket with Great Value Butter Cooking Spray.
13. Place rolls seam-side down in a single layer — do not let them touch.
14. Spray the tops of the rolls lightly with cooking spray.
15. Air fry for 10–12 minutes, flipping carefully halfway through, until golden and crispy all over.

**Finish**

16. Remove immediately and serve hot. Rice paper rolls lose their crispiness within 10–15 minutes — eat straight from the air fryer.

### Storage

- ❄️ Filling keeps refrigerated up to 2 days. Assemble and air fry fresh each time.
- ♨️ Reheat in air fryer at 180°C / 350°F for 3–4 minutes to restore crispiness. Do not microwave.

**Filling:**

🐟 Great Value Chunk Light Tuna in Water — 112g drained (2 cans)

🧀 Great Value Shredded Mozzarella — 42g

### Directions

🍅 Finely dice Roma tomato into ~0.5cm pieces. Pat completely dry with paper towels — press firmly. Excess tomato moisture is the main enemy of crispy rolls. If too wet, the rice paper will steam from the inside and go soggy rather than crispy.

🌪️ Preheat air fryer to 200°C / 390°F for 3 minutes.

🌯 Place rolls seam-side down in a single layer — do not let them touch.

🔥 Air fry 10–12 minutes — flip carefully halfway through using tongs or a wide spatula. Spray the flipped side lightly.

✅ Done when deep golden and crispy all over — the mozzarella will have melted inside and may peek through the seam slightly.

**Finish**

✅ Remove immediately and serve hot. Rice paper rolls lose their crispiness within 10–15 minutes — eat straight from the air fryer.

## Recipe 43 — Strawberry Vanilla Soufflé Protein Pancakes ☁️

**🏋️ Gym Day · Breakfast · High Protein · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · Stovetop with Lid**

> ⚠️ **Cook's note:** These are tall, cloud-like, and jiggly. They require a stovetop pan with a tight-fitting lid and patience on low heat. Eat them immediately — soufflé pancakes deflate within 10–15 minutes as they cool. Do not attempt these if you're rushed.

### Ingredients

- 🥚 Great Value Large White Eggs — 150g (2 yolks + 3 whites, separated)

**Prep — Read fully before starting. Timing matters.**

3. Separate eggs carefully — 2 yolks into a medium bowl, 3 whites into a large clean dry bowl. ⚠️ Any yolk in the whites will prevent them from whipping. Chill the whites in the freezer for 10 minutes.

11. ⚠️ Use the batter immediately — do not let it sit or it will deflate.

17. Transfer to a plate immediately.
18. Top generously with sliced fresh strawberries.
19. Drizzle Previsto Sugar Free Hazelnut Syrup over everything.
20. Serve and eat within 10 minutes — these will deflate as they cool. That's normal. Enjoy them at their peak!

## Recipe 53 — Strawberry Custard Protein Porridge 🍓☕

**1 Day · Gym Day Breakfast · High Protein · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · Sweet · Stovetop + Blender**

> 🔄 **Updated:** Cornstarch added as thickener — gives a proper silky custard set, protects eggs from curdling, and makes the cook significantly more forgiving.

---

### Ingredients

🍓 Great Value Whole Strawberries (frozen) — 120g

🥚 Great Value Large White Eggs — 100g (2 large)

🥤 Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard 100% Whey Protein — 16g (½ scoop)

🥛 NIDO Whole Milk Powder — 13g (2 tbsp)

🌽 Great Value Corn Starch — 8g (1 tbsp)

💧 Water — 220ml

☕ Great Value No Calorie Sweetener — 2 packets

🍂 Ground Cinnamon — ¼ tsp

🧂 Great Value Iodized Salt — 1 tiny pinch

🥄 Great Value Butter Cooking Spray — 1 spray

**Finish:**

🍮 Previsto Sugar Free Caramel Syrup — 10g (drizzle)

🌰 Previsto Sugar Free Hazelnut Syrup — 10g (drizzle)

---

### Directions

**Prep — Strawberry Base (3 min)**

🍓 Place frozen strawberries in a microwave-safe bowl. Microwave 60–90 seconds until fully thawed and syrupy — they will release a deep red juice. Do not drain.

🍓 Transfer thawed strawberries and all their juice into the blender. Blend on high for 20–25 seconds until completely smooth with no chunks. You should have a thick, vibrant pink purée. Set aside.

**Prep — Custard Mixture (3 min)**

🌽 In a medium bowl, whisk cornstarch with 3 tbsp (45ml) of the cold water until completely dissolved and lump-free — this is your slurry. It must be fully smooth before anything else is added or you will get starch lumps in the final custard.

🔥 Place over medium heat. Stir continuously with a silicone spatula using wide figure-8 motions, scraping the bottom and sides constantly.

🔥 After 3–4 minutes the mixture will begin to thicken noticeably — keep stirring. Bring to a gentle simmer — you will see slow lazy bubbles breaking the surface. This is the starch fully activating.

🔥 Once you see the first gentle bubbles, cook for exactly 60 more seconds while stirring continuously — this cooks out the raw starch taste completely.

🔥 Remove from heat immediately after the 60 seconds.

**Finish**

⏳ Let the custard sit off the heat for 30 seconds — it will thicken slightly more from residual heat and the temperature will drop just enough to safely add the whey.

🥤 Stir in the pre-dissolved whey protein quickly and thoroughly — stir for 20–30 seconds until fully incorporated with no streaks.

🍮 Drizzle Previsto Sugar Free Caramel Syrup over the top in a thin spiral.

## Recipe 52 — Multigrain Anise & Cumin Oat Crackers 🫙

**Batch · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · Wheat-Free · Gluten-Free · Multigrain · Oven**

> 🔄 **Updated:** Multigrain base (oats + flax + chia), whole anise seeds, ground cumin ¼ tsp, cling film replaced with parchment paper throughout.

---

### Ingredients — Makes ~20 crackers

🌾 Great Value Whole Grain Old Fashioned Oats — 100g, ground to fine flour

🌱 Great Value Organic Chia Seeds — 10g *(whole — add texture and bind)*

🌾 Great Value Organic Ground Cold Milled Flax Seed — 15g *(binder + whole grain)*

🌿 Anise Seeds — 1½ tsp (whole) ⚠️ Estimated

🫙 Badia Ground Cumin — ¼ tsp (ground)

💧 Water — 60–70ml (ice cold)

🥄 Great Value Canola Oil — 14g (1 tbsp)

🧂 Great Value Iodized Salt — ½ tsp + extra pinch for topping

🌿 Baking powder — 1g (¼ tsp) ⚠️ Estimated

---

### Directions

**Prep — Multigrain Flour (3 min)**

🌾 Pulse 100g oats in a blender for 25–30 seconds until a very fine flour forms — no visible whole oat flakes remaining. Tap blender and check.

🌾 Add ground flaxseed, baking powder, and ground cumin to the oat flour. Whisk together until evenly combined. Ground cumin distributes uniformly throughout the dough, giving a subtle even warmth in every bite.

🌿 Add whole anise seeds directly to the dry mixture — do not grind them. Stir to distribute evenly. The whole seeds will give visible texture and bursts of flavor in every cracker, just like store-bought multigrain crackers.

🌱 Add whole chia seeds last and stir through — they will hydrate slightly during the rest and act as an additional binder.

**Make the Dough (3 min)**

🥄 Add canola oil to the dry mixture. Use your fingertips to rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse sand — no oil pools remaining.

🧂 Add salt and stir through.

💧 Add ice cold water 1 tbsp at a time — start with 50ml, stir with a fork after each addition. Stop the moment the dough just comes together and holds when pressed. It should not be sticky. If too dry, add water ½ tsp at a time.

**Rest — Parchment Method (15 min)**

🔪 Divide dough into 2 equal portions.

📄 Place one portion onto a sheet of parchment paper. Place a second sheet of parchment paper directly on top.

⏳ Rest the wrapped portions in the fridge for 15 minutes — this firms the fat, hydrates the chia seeds fully, and makes the dough dramatically easier to roll thin without cracking. Do not skip this step.

**Roll & Cut (5 min)**

🔪 Preheat oven to 160°C / 320°F. Line a large baking tray with parchment paper.

🔪 Remove one dough portion from the fridge. Keep it between the two sheets of parchment paper — roll directly through the top sheet using a rolling pin.

🔪 Roll as thin as possible — target 1.5–2mm. The parchment prevents sticking without any flour needed. The seeds will create slight bumps — press firmly through them.

🔪 Peel back the top parchment sheet. Cut into rounds using a glass or cookie cutter (~5cm diameter) — or cut into squares with a knife or pizza cutter. Re-roll scraps between fresh parchment.

🔪 Use a thin spatula to transfer crackers to the lined baking tray, spacing slightly apart.

🔪 Prick each cracker 3–4 times with a fork — essential to prevent puffing.

🧂 Sprinkle a tiny pinch of salt over each cracker before baking.

🔪 Repeat with the second dough portion.

**Bake**

🔥 Bake at 160°C for 20–25 minutes until edges are golden and centers look dry and matte — not shiny.

⚠️ Check at 18 minutes. The anise will become very fragrant — this is the signal they are almost done. Watch closely from this point.

🔥 Turn oven off. Leave crackers inside with the door slightly ajar for 10 more minutes — this dries them out completely and delivers the snap.

**Finish**

✅ Transfer to a wire rack. Do not stack or store until completely cool — at least 20 minutes. They crisp up fully as they cool.

✅ The snap test: pick one up and break it. It should snap cleanly with no bending. If it bends, return the batch to the oven at 150°C for 5 more minutes.

---

### Storage

❄️ Store in an airtight container at room temperature — up to 5 days.

⚠️ Do not refrigerate — moisture will soften them. If they lose crispness, spread on a tray and bake at 150°C for 5 minutes to restore.

## Recipe 31 — Cast Iron Teriyaki-Glazed Beef Liver with Caramelised Onions & Green Pepper 🍳

### Ingredients

🧅 Red Onions — 100g, sliced into thin half-rings

🫑 Green Bell Pepper — 100g, sliced into thin strips

## Recipe 55 — Spiced Air Fryer Beef Liver with Roasted Green Pepper & Mini Potato 🔥

**1 Day · Rest Day Lunch · High Protein · Iron-Rich · IBS-Safe · Low FODMAP · Air Fryer**

---

### Ingredients

🫀 Skylark Frozen Beef Liver, 4 Slices, 1 lb — 224g (2 slices), fully thawed

🥔 Russet Potatoes — 75g raw, cut into thin wedges (~1cm thick)

🫑 Green Bell Pepper — 150g (~1 large), sliced into strips

🧅 Red Onions — 80g, sliced into rings

🥄 Great Value Canola Oil — 8g (for marinade)

🥄 Great Value Butter Cooking Spray — for air fryer basket and vegetables

🍋 Lemon juice — 25ml (~1½ tbsp) ⚠️ Estimated

🫙 Badia Ground Cumin — 1½ tsp

🫙 Smoked Paprika — 1½ tsp

🫙 Ground Coriander — ¾ tsp

🫙 Ginger Powder — ½ tsp

🧂 Great Value Iodized Salt — 1 tsp

🫙 Badia Ground Black Pepper — ½ tsp

🌿 Fresh parsley — small handful, chopped to finish ⚠️ Estimated

---

### Directions

**Prep**

🔪 Thaw both liver slices completely. Pat completely dry with paper towels. Score lightly on both sides.

🔪 Combine canola oil, lemon juice, cumin, smoked paprika, coriander, ginger, salt, and black pepper into a wet marinade paste. Coat liver thoroughly on both sides. Marinate 15–20 minutes at room temperature.

🔪 Cut potato into thin wedges (~1cm thick). Slice pepper into strips. Slice onion into rings.

**Cook the Potato First**

🌪️ Preheat air fryer to 200°C / 400°F for 3 minutes. Spray basket. Add potato wedges in a single layer, spray tops lightly.

🔥 Air fry at 200°C for 12 minutes, flipping halfway. Remove and tent with foil.

**Cook the Vegetables**

🥄 Spray basket again. Add pepper strips and onion rings in a single layer, spray tops lightly.

🔥 Air fry at 190°C / 375°F for 6–7 minutes until softened with light char. Remove and set aside.

**Cook the Liver**

🥄 Spray basket well. Place both liver slices flat — do not stack or overlap.

🔥 Air fry at 190°C / 375°F for 4 minutes. Flip carefully.

🔥 Air fry another 3–4 minutes. ⚠️ Check at 7 minutes total — inside should be just barely pink. Do NOT exceed 8 minutes total or liver turns rubbery and bitter.

**Assemble**

✅ Plate both liver slices. Arrange roasted pepper, onion, and potato wedges alongside.

✅ Squeeze fresh lemon juice over everything. Finish with chopped fresh parsley. Serve immediately.

---
